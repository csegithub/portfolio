Project: MIT App Inventor - Atari Breakout

Course: Computer Science and Engineering

September 20, 2017 to October 6, 2017

My partner and I had brainstormed project ideas on Day #1 and decided upon the classic game of Atari Breakout to recreate using MIT App Inventor. We used a multitude of sprites, and a variety of algorithms and were able to navigate App Inventor with the help of of our peers, our teacher, and of course the internet. There were some difficult aspects. We were unable to allow users to angle the ball correctly, but eventually, made up for it by adding more levels and more challenges. We learnt to call on different aspects at different times and became experts on variable control, in MIT App Inventor at least. I worked mostly on the transitions between the screens, variable activation, and the basic layout/structure for the game. Our class was also required to create a notebook, in which we documented our progress and better showcased our end result: the project. The notebook consisted of much professional writing that had to be clear and concise, which was determined by the rubric.
My partner and I received an “A” on this project.

Dublin High School
