Project: MIT App Inventor - Atari Breakout

Course: Computer Science and Engineering

September 20, 2017 to October 6, 2017

My partner and I had brainstormed project ideas on Day #1 and decided upon the classic game of Atari Breakout to recreate using MIT App Inventor. We used a multitude of sprites, and a variety of algorithms and were able to navigate App Inventor with the help of of our peers, our teacher, and of course the internet. There were some difficult aspects. We were unable to allow users to angle the ball correctly, but eventually, made up for it by adding more levels and more challenges. We learnt to call on different aspects at different times and became experts on variable control, in MIT App Inventor at least. I worked mostly on the transitions between the screens, variable activation, and the basic layout/structure for the game. Our class was also required to create a notebook, in which we documented our progress and better showcased our end result: the project. The notebook consisted of much professional writing that had to be clear and concise, which was determined by the rubric.
My partner and I received an “A” on this project.

Project: Python - American Humane Association Image Manipluation

Course: Computer Science and Engineering

March 6, 2018 to March 25, 2018

This project revolved around changing/manipulating the appearance of an image based on the requirements posed by a variety of fictitous clients (client options provided in project instructions). My partner and I had chosen Client #1: A Cause. On Day #1 we chose to represent the American Humane Association for our cause. We focused on three main image manipulations, overlapping an image over the original, adding a border of circles and and adding a soild-colored border. We also decided to add the watermark of the AHA. Just like any project, there were some difficult aspects; At first we were unable to figure out how to have the code iterate over an entire file without interruption, but the code was unable to return all images in a folder as well. Through hours of experimentation, I finally understood came to the solution by researching how certain PIL.Image methods affect others. I worked mostly the completion of all three borders and fixed the ability for the code to iteratre over an entire folder. Our class was also required to create a notebook or sort to document our progress and we showcase our end result. The notebook consisted of much professional writing that had to be clear and concise, which was determined by the rubric.
My partner and I received an “A” on this project.
